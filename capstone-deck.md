# Slide 1 - Irish Flute D4

L2 engineering packet for a keyless D4 Irish flute.

# Slide 2 - Project Intent

Create a measurable first prototype with open-pipe physics, clear shop gates, and validation rows for every tuning claim.

# Slide 3 - Acoustic Model

Open-open cylindrical pipe, `f = c/(2*L_eff)`, with ordinary open-end correction at both ends.

# Slide 4 - Build Sequence

Scrap bore, tonewood bore, turn from bore axis, cut embouchure, measure fundamental, drill and tune one hole at a time.

# Slide 5 - Packet Files

README, design, BOM, sourcing, cut list, validation worksheet, drawings starter, CAD notes, risks, and photo plan.

# Slide 6 - Materials

Hardwood blank, scrap blank, long 3/4 in bit, optional reamer, tone-hole bits, bore finish, exterior finish.

# Slide 7 - Drawings

The current drawing is an L2 layout starter. Embouchure and fixture drawings remain open until shop measurements exist.

# Slide 8 - Risks

Top risks are bore wander, embouchure effective-length error, and irreversible tone-hole over-opening.

# Slide 9 - Validation

Pitch, bore, wall, and ergonomic checks are captured in `validation.csv`.

# Slide 10 - Sourceability

Supplier rows are not verified. Prices, lead times, and stock must be checked before purchasing.

# Slide 11 - Readiness

L2 scaffold only. Not build-ready until the first prototype is measured.

# Slide 12 - Next Actions

Verify tooling, run scrap bore, cut embouchure on scrap, then update the workbook and validation sheet from measured data.
