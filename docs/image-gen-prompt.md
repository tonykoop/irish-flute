# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A keyless wooden Irish flute presented as a single finished instrument, photographed as a real physical object: a slender transverse flute roughly the length of a forearm-and-a-half, turned from dark close-grained hardwood (grenadilla or ebonite-like deep brown-black) with a warm satin-oiled sheen and fine straight grain running the length of the body. The defining feature is the simple keyless layout — one oval embouchure hole near the closed head end with a crisply hand-cut, slightly undercut blowing edge, and a single straight row of six round open tone holes along the front of the body, no metal keywork or pads anywhere. The bore reads as a clean cylindrical tube; the open foot end is plain and unadorned. Subtle hand-finishing cues at the head and foot suggest a turned, oiled wooden tube rather than a machined part. Clean neutral light-grey studio background, soft directional studio light grazing the body to bring out grain and the carved embouchure, 3/4 product-photography angle, approximately 50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same keyless wooden Irish flute resting across a worn wooden workbench in a small luthier's workshop, alongside a long boring reamer and a hand chisel suggestive of the embouchure-cutting stage, wood shavings nearby. Warm natural window light from the side, shallow depth of field with the carved embouchure and first tone holes in sharp focus and the tools softly blurred behind. Environmental storytelling of a hand-built session flute; concept visualization only, no measurement markings.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the flute's signature voicing detail: the oval embouchure hole and the closely spaced first one or two open tone holes, showing the crisp hand-cut blowing edge, the gentle interior undercut of the embouchure window, the satin-oiled dark hardwood grain, and the clean rounded rim of each open hole bored straight through the wall. Raking light across the surface to reveal grain, tool sheen, and the slight chamfer of the cut edges, high detail, shallow focus, no measurements or callouts.

## Family Group Prompt

A graduated set of keyless wooden Irish flutes of the same design lying parallel on a neutral surface, ordered by length to imply different keys of the same family — the realized D4 prototype as the reference instrument among slightly longer and shorter siblings. All share the identical visual language: dark oiled hardwood tubes, a single oval embouchure near each head, and one straight row of six open front tone holes, no keywork. Soft even studio light, top-down 3/4 angle, family-portrait composition, photorealistic, no text labels.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines or measurement callouts; no metal keys, pads, rods, or saxophone-style keywork; no impossible bore or hole geometry; if hands appear, anatomically plausible with a realistic transverse flute hold.
